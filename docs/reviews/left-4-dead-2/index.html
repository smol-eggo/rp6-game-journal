Experience

Left 4 Dead 2 was one of the biggest surprises I’ve tested.

Running natively on Linux, the game delivered an excellent experience on the RP6. During my testing I was able to play while maintaining around 60 FPS, with only occasional dips during particularly chaotic moments.

But numbers aren’t why it earns a 🍇.

The controls felt responsive, the gameplay remained fluid, and after a few minutes I completely forgot I was supposed to be evaluating performance.

I was just trying to survive another horde.

For a game released in 2009, it still feels remarkably good to pick up and play on a handheld.

smol-eggo’s Verdict

Valve may not have had ARM handhelds in mind when they made Left 4 Dead 2, but it certainly feels at home here.

Native Linux support makes setup refreshingly simple, and the end result is one of the easiest recommendations in this journal.

Grab three friends…

Or trust the AI.

Either way, you’ll probably lose track of time.

Tested by Jens on the Retroid Pocket 6 running Armada.

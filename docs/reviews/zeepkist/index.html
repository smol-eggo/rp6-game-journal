Experience

Zeepkist was a pleasant surprise.

On simpler tracks, performance remained smooth and the game was genuinely enjoyable. Racing downhill while shaving milliseconds off my best time felt great on a handheld, making it easy to squeeze in a few quick sessions.

The only downside came with larger community-made tracks. Once the environments became more detailed and crowded, noticeable stuttering started to creep in. It never made the game completely unplayable, but it was enough to remind me that the RP6 does have its limits.

For the official tracks and smaller creations though, it’s a fantastic fit.

smol-eggo’s Verdict

Not every game has to be perfect to earn a place on your handheld.

If you’re happy sticking to smaller tracks, Zeepkist is well worth bringing along.
